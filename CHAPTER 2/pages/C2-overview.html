"A Walk to Remember" is a novel by American author Nicholas Sparks, first published in 1999. It tells the story of Landon Carter, a high school senior, and Jamie Sullivan, the daughter of the town's Baptist minister. Landon is a popular but rebellious teenager, while Jamie is a quiet, kind-hearted girl who is often mocked by her peers for her religious beliefs and plain appearance.

Despite their differences, Landon and Jamie become unlikely friends when Landon is forced to participate in the school play and Jamie is his unlikely partner. As they spend more time together, Landon begins to see Jamie in a new light and falls in love with her. However, their relationship faces numerous challenges, including opposition from Landon's friends and Jamie's deteriorating health.

In 2002, "A Walk to Remember" was adapted into a film of the same name starring Shane West as Landon Carter and Mandy Moore as Jamie Sullivan. While the movie retains the core elements of the novel's storyline, there are some differences in the adaptation, as is often the case with book-to-movie adaptations.
